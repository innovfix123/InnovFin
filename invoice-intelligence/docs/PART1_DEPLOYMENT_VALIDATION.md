# Part 1 — Gmail-Native Invoice Gateway: Deployment & Validation Record

Scope: **Part 1 only** — detect invoice emails in company Gmail mailboxes and forward them to
one central mailbox, labeled `Invoices`, using Gmail-native filters. OCR/AI is **Part 2** and is
explicitly out of scope here.

## 1. What was deployed (test environment)
| Mailbox | Role | Type | Filters |
|---|---|---|---|
| `satyamsahu0877@gmail.com` | Source | consumer | 10 (1 forward + 7 tier labels + 1 review) |
| `sat211053@gmail.com` | Source | consumer | 10 |
| `satyam@innovfix.in` | Central | **Workspace** | 1 (invoice-signal → label `Invoices`, never spam) |

Filters are generated by `python cli.py gmail-build` into `build/filters/` and imported per
mailbox. Forwarding to central was verified once per source account.

## 2. The key fix made during validation (central labeling)
**Problem observed live:** forwarded invoices reached Central but were **not labeled** `Invoices`
and some landed in **Spam**.
**Root cause (confirmed by real `From` headers):** forwarded Gmail shows the **original sender**
in `From` (e.g. `sahu.vaibhav1106@gmail.com`), not the source account. The original central filter
matched `from:(source addresses)`, so it never matched forwarded mail.
**Fix (config-driven, no rewrite):** central `routing_rules.use_invoice_signals: true` — the
central filter now matches the **invoice-signal query** (same detection as the forward filter) and
applies `Invoices` + **Never send to Spam**. Robust regardless of `From`.

## 4. Known native limits (structural — closed in Part 2, not bugs)
- **ZIP / non-PDF containers:** Gmail cannot read inside a ZIP → a generically-named zipped invoice
  is not detected.
- **Image-only invoices** (jpg/png) with no invoice keyword/filename → not detected natively.
- **No de-duplication:** a re-sent invoice is forwarded again (2 copies in central).
- **Spam:** anything Gmail classifies as spam in a *source* mailbox is never forwarded.
- **Forwarding caps:** consumer ~500/day per mailbox; Workspace 10,000/day (see
  [GMAIL_NATIVE_BEHAVIOUR.md](GMAIL_NATIVE_BEHAVIOUR.md)).

These are exactly the cases Part 2 (attachment collection + OCR + AI) is designed to handle.

## 5. Production rollout (when real mailboxes are provided)
1. Replace the test addresses in `config/mailboxes.yaml` with the real source + central mailboxes
   (keep `central.routing_rules.use_invoice_signals: true`).
2. `python cli.py mailbox-check` (validate) → `python cli.py gmail-build` (regenerate).
3. Per source mailbox (owner can do it on their own laptop, no shared passwords): verify forwarding
   to central → delete any old filters → import that mailbox's XML.
4. Central: delete any old filter → import the central XML.
5. Live-test a couple of mailboxes; confirm `Invoices` labeling + zero misses.
- If the company has **Google Workspace admin access**, prefer a single Admin content-compliance /
  routing rule to deploy to all mailboxes at once (no per-mailbox import) — per-mailbox XML remains
  the fallback for consumer accounts.
